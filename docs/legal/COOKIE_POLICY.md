## 2. What Are Cookies?

**Cookies** are small text files stored on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They help websites:
- Remember your preferences and settings
- Authenticate your identity
- Analyze how you use the site
- Improve performance and user experience

**Similar Technologies** include:
- **Local Storage:** Data stored in your browser (larger than cookies)
- **Session Storage:** Temporary data cleared when you close the browser
- **Web Beacons:** Tiny images that track page views
- **Device Fingerprinting:** Identifying devices based on configurations

## 5. How to Manage Cookies

### 5.1 Cookie Consent Banner

On your first visit, you'll see a cookie consent banner with options:
- **Accept All:** Enable all cookies (essential, functional, analytics, performance)
- **Reject Non-Essential:** Only essential cookies (authentication, security)
- **Customize:** Choose which cookie categories to accept

Your preferences are saved for **1 year**. You can change them anytime by clicking "Cookie Settings" in the footer.

### 5.2 Browser Settings

You can control cookies directly in your browser:

**Google Chrome:**
1. Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
2. Choose: "Block third-party cookies" or "Block all cookies"
3. Manage site-specific permissions

**Mozilla Firefox:**
1. Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
2. Choose: "Block cookies and site data"
3. Set exceptions for trusted sites

**Safari:**
1. Preferences → Privacy
2. Enable "Block all cookies"
3. Manage website data

**Microsoft Edge:**
1. Settings → Cookies and site permissions
2. Choose: "Block third-party cookies" or "Block all cookies"

**Mobile Browsers:**
- Settings vary by device; check browser help documentation

**Note:** Blocking essential cookies will prevent you from logging in or using the Platform.

### 5.3 Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers support "Do Not Track" signals. We respect DNT preferences:
- **DNT Enabled:** We will not set analytics or performance cookies
- **DNT Disabled:** Standard cookie policy applies

**How to Enable DNT:**
- **Chrome:** Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies → "Send 'Do Not Track' request"
- **Firefox:** Options → Privacy & Security → "Send websites a 'Do Not Track' signal"
- **Safari:** Preferences → Privacy → "Ask websites not to track me"

## 8. Cookies and Your Privacy

### 8.1 Personal Data in Cookies

**Most cookies do NOT contain personal data.** They store:
- Random identifiers (e.g., `GA1.2.123456789.1234567890`)
- Preference settings (e.g., `theme=dark`)
- Session tokens (e.g., `session_abc123xyz`)

**Exceptions:**
- Authentication cookies contain **encrypted user IDs** (not readable by third parties)
- CSRF tokens protect against attacks (random strings, no PII)

### 8.2 Data Sharing

**We do NOT:**
- Sell cookie data to third parties
- Use cookies for cross-site tracking (except Google Analytics)
- Share cookie data with advertisers

**Third-party services** (Firebase, Sentry) process cookie data under **Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)** and comply with GDPR/POPIA.

### 8.3 Security

**Cookie Security Measures:**
- **HttpOnly:** Prevents JavaScript access (XSS protection)
- **Secure:** Only transmitted over HTTPS (encrypted)
- **SameSite:** Restricts cross-site cookie sending (CSRF protection)
- **Expiration:** Cookies auto-delete after set duration
